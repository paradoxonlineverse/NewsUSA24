Neil Young Revives "My Boy" in Rare Live Performance

Music 2024

A Deep Cut Resurfaces

In a recent live performance, legendary musician Neil Young surprised audiences by performing "My Boy," a poignant track from his 1985 album Old Ways. This song, a heartfelt ode to his son Zeke, had not been featured in Young's live sets for decades, making its resurgence a notable event for fans and music historians alike.

The Significance of "My Boy"

"My Boy" stands out in Young's discography for its deeply personal lyrics and emotional resonance. Written during a period when Young was exploring country influences, the song reflects his experiences and sentiments as a father. In a 2020 post on his Archives website, Young described "My Boy" as "probably the most soulful recording" I have ever made, underscoring its importance in his body of work.

Historical Context and Recording

The track was recorded at the House of David studio in Nashville on January 28, 1983, during sessions that would culminate in the Old Ways album. This period marked Young's deliberate shift towards country music, collaborating with artists like Willie Nelson and Waylon Jennings. The inclusion of "My Boy" in the album added a layer of introspection and familial warmth to the collection.

Recent Live Performance Details

Young's decision to revisit "My Boy" in his recent concert has been met with enthusiasm from fans and critics. The performance not only showcases his enduring connection to the song but also offers audiences a rare glimpse into a more intimate facet of his musical journey. The rendition was characterized by its stripped-down arrangement, allowing the emotional weight of the lyrics to take center stage.

Legacy and Continued Relevance

The revival of "My Boy" serves as a testament to Neil Young's ability to connect with audiences through sincere and personal storytelling. As he continues to perform and share his music, songs like "My Boy" highlight the timeless nature of his artistry and the enduring impact of his contributions to the music world.
