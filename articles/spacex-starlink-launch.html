SpaceX Successfully Launches 23 Starlink Satellites on Falcon 9

Space Technology May 24, 2025

Mission Overview

On May 24, 2025, SpaceX launched 23 Starlink V2 Mini satellites aboard a Falcon 9 rocket from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in Florida. The mission, designated Starlink 12-22, lifted off at 1:19 p.m. EDT (17:19 UTC), marking the company's 45th Starlink launch of the year.

Booster Details and Landing

The Falcon 9 first-stage booster, tail number B1069, was on its 24th flight, having previously supported missions including SpaceX's 24th Commercial Resupply Services flight to the International Space Station and multiple Starlink deployments. Approximately eight minutes after liftoff, B1069 successfully landed on the droneship "A Shortfall of Gravitas" stationed in the Atlantic Ocean. This landing marked the 110th touchdown for this vessel and the 451st booster landing to date.

Payload Specifications

The payload comprised 23 Starlink V2 Mini satellites, 13 of which are equipped with Direct to Cell capabilities. These satellites are part of SpaceX's initiative to provide global cellular connectivity directly from space, enhancing communication services in remote and underserved areas.

Weather Conditions

The 45th Weather Squadron had forecasted an 80% chance of favorable weather at the opening of the launch window, with conditions expected to deteriorate as the day progressed. Isolated showers were anticipated during the early afternoon, with more robust thunderstorms likely developing inland by mid to late afternoon.

Conclusion

The successful deployment of these 23 Starlink satellites continues SpaceX's rapid expansion of its satellite internet constellation, aiming to provide high-speed internet access across the globe. The inclusion of Direct to Cell capabilities signifies a significant step towards bridging connectivity gaps in remote regions.
