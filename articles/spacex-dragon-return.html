SpaceX Dragon Cargo Capsule Creates Spectacle Over Southern California During ISS Return

Space May 24, 2025

Spectacular Reentry Captivates Southern California

On the evening of Saturday, May 24, 2025, residents across Southern California witnessed a remarkable celestial event as SpaceX's Dragon cargo capsule re-entered Earth's atmosphere. The spacecraft, returning from a resupply mission to the International Space Station (ISS), was visible as a luminous streak traversing the night sky.

Mission Overview: CRS-32 Returns to Earth

The Dragon capsule was part of SpaceX's 32nd Commercial Resupply Services (CRS-32) mission for NASA. After delivering approximately 6,700 pounds of space supplies, scientific experiments, and equipment to the ISS, the spacecraft undocked and commenced its journey back to Earth.

Sonic Boom and Visual Phenomenon

As the Dragon capsule re-entered the atmosphere, it generated a sonic boom that was heard across various parts of Southern California. Eyewitnesses reported a loud, thunderous sound coinciding with the sight of a spacecraft reentry streaking across the sky.

Splashdown and Recovery Operations

The capsule concluded its mission with a splashdown recovery approximately 25 miles west of Camp Pendleton. Recovery teams promptly secured the space capsule, ensuring the safe retrieval of valuable scientific data and equipment.

Public Engagement and Future Missions

The visibility of the Dragon capsule's reentry provided a unique opportunity for space exploration engagement.
