U.S. Federal Judge Orders Return of Wrongfully Deported Guatemalan Asylum Seeker

Immigration May 24, 2025

Overview of the Judicial Ruling

On May 24, 2025, U.S. District Judge Brian Murphy mandated that the Trump administration facilitate the return of a Guatemalan asylum seeker, referred to by the initials OCG, who was deported in violation of a court order. OCG, a gay man, had fled Guatemala due to homophobic violence and sought asylum in the United States. Despite a judicial order protecting him from deportation, he was removed to Mexico, where he had previously suffered rape and kidnapping. Judge Murphy criticized the deportation as lacking "any semblance of due process" and emphasized that OCG posed no security threat.

Context of the Deportation

OCG's deportation proceedings occurred despite a prior court ruling recognizing the risk of persecution or torture he faced if returned to Guatemala. After being sent to Mexico, he was later returned to Guatemala, where he has been living in hiding, fearing for his safety. The case highlights the challenges faced by LGBTQ+ asylum seekers and raises questions about the enforcement of immigration laws under the Trump administration.

Broader Implications

This incident is part of a series of controversial deportations during the Trump administration's second term. Other cases include the deportation of Venezuelan asylum seeker Andry José Hernández Romero to a Salvadoran prison based on misidentified tattoos and the wrongful deportation of Maryland resident Kilmar Abrego Garcia to El Salvador, despite a protective court order.
